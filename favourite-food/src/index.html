A Tasty Chicken Shawarma!

Shawarmas origin


Shawarma is a Middle Eastern dish that originated in the Levant during the Ottoman Empire, consisting of meat that is cut into thin slices, stacked in an inverted cone, and roasted on a slow-turning vertical spit.


How to make a Chicken Shawarma ?

Ingredients:

  • Boneless, Skinless Chicken Thighs
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Olive Oil
  • Lemon Juice
  • Shawarma Spice Blend
  • Chopped Fresh Parsley
  • Cherry Tomatoe

Preparation


Step One
Make the Marinade: In a large bowl, mix together the olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, cumin, paprika, coriander, turmeric, cinnamon, cayenne, salt and pepper to make the shawarma spice marinade.

Step Two
Marinate the chicken: Add the chicken thighs and onion to the bowl and toss well to coat evenly with the marinade. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour or overnight for maximum flavor.

Step Three
Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 425Â°F. Transfer the marinated chicken and onions to a large baking sheet.

Step Four
Bake the chicken: Bake for 30-40 minutes, turning once halfway through, until the chicken is cooked through and browned. For extra crispy edges, broil on high for the last 2-3 minutes

Step Five
Slice the meat: Remove from the oven and let the chicken rest for a few minutes. Slice into thin shawarma-style shavings.

Step Six
Serve: Serve on warm pita bread with toppings like shredded lettuce, chopped tomatoes, cucumbers, and garlic sauce. You can also serve it on a plate with rice, salads, and hummus on the side.
